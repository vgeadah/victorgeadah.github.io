Victor Geadah

I'm a PhD Candidate in Applied & Computational Mathematics at Princeton University, working under the supervision of Jonathan Pillow at the Princeton Neuroscience Institute.

My research interests span computational neuroscience, dynamical systems and probabilistic machine learning. I'm generally interested in bridging normative accounts with descriptive approaches to analyzing data, as to help close the loop in neuroscience between theoretical insights into dynamical computation and experiments. Currently, I focus on exploring how latent processes evolve over time and influence decision-making, and how we can infer from experimental data what these control processes are.

Bio: Before Princeton, I studied applied mathematics at Cambridge (master's student, Part III), and mathematics at the Université de Montréal (BSc), where I worked with Guillaume Lajoie on dynamic coding in recurrent neural networks.
